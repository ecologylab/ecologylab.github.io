Live streaming is an emerging form of social media, which is fostering unique participatory online experiences through combination of live media forms. We have observed that live streams, through the unique participatory experiences they afford, often serve as online places supporting the formation of online communities. In our work, we aim to build a further understanding of contexts in which live streaming is enabling shared community formation and experiences. We are also currently working to design and develop new tools to support expressive and participatory live media experiences. In an upcoming project, we are working to integrate our information composition and live media research to design a composition environment to support the collaborative formation of live media places.

Our original research in the space of live media focused on the emergence of video game streaming. We are currently working to apply our findings from this investigation to online education contexts. While MOOCs, on edX, Coursera, and Udacity, have expanded the reach and scale of online education, their prior modalities struggle to engender participatory learning. Through the design and evaluation of new tools, such as live media composition, we aim to help online students and instructors create engaging online places for participatory learning experiences.
