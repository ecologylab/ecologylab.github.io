Play is an essential part of the human experience. Play brings us together and enables us to connect emotionally with one another. Games in particular present us with a unique context in which we can take creative action, explore our emotions, learn, interact, and live. We see digital games as a compelling domain in which to explore human-computer and human-human interaction.

Through play, gamers are developing unique skills, experiences, and relationships. We find that this makes gamers an interesting demographic for study. Drawing from their unique background, gamers can provide new perspectives and insights on problems traditionally studied in other domains.

Furthermore, a complex culture has emerged around games. New social media forms, such as live-streaming, are enabling gamers to share their play experiences and form new communities. We are interested in understanding these new media forms and play communities.

We aim to leverage our understanding of play and games culture to not only create new meaningful play experiences, but to inform our understanding and design in other contexts.
