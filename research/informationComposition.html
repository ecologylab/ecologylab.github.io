From Google to Facebook to PowerPoint, collections of content are typically presented in restrictive, linear forms. Digital content becomes highly intertwined.

Visual thinking connects perception with higher order cognitive operations, such as active exploration, analysis, synthesis, and abstraction. Expression and visual thinking are intertwined in curation, which in art is defined as the spatial assemblage of elements to conceptualize and express context in meaningful exhibits.

To engage in information-based ideation, people need to be able to curate content in a form that enables expressing relationships and connections. We characterizes free-form web curation as a spontaneous, improvisational, and divergent creative process that involves choosing elements, sketching, and writing, and assembling them spatially.

Information composition is a holistic, free-form medium for curating digital content as a visually and semantically connected whole. InfoComposer, combinFormation, and CollageMachine were prior information composition systems.

IdeaMâché

IdeaMâché is a web browser and cloud based free-form web curation system. In IdeaMâché, users gather clippings, which automatically become enriched with web semantics and so are represented as rich clippings. The semantics include descriptions, titles, and iconic images, as well as authors, categories, and citations of articles and patents, and price, ratings and bestseller rankins of products from vendors such as Amazon and HomeDepot. Visual operations on clippings can create a visual sense of connectedness among curated elements.
