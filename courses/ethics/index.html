welcome [cs 377]

Computing is such a growing aspect of society. Computing's impacts on society can be positive or negative. What's a computer scientist to do?

Computer scientists have opportunities to make the world a better place. Or worse. The ACM Code of Ethics and Professional Conduct begins, "Computing professionals' actions change the world. To act responsibly, [we] should reflect upon the wider impacts of [our] work, consistently supporting the public good."

What an immense power and responsibility. We need ways to think about what good is. How can we know? There is not one way.

This course introduces several ethical frameworks for thinking about what good is, about what ethical behavior means. We use these frameworks to consider and discuss current events in computing, which can be loaded with ethical dilemmas, such as social media, misinformation, AI, bias, intellectual property, and privacy.

Note: this course is English language reading and writing intensive. Critical thinking is vital.
