welcome csce 667: seminar in
human-centered computing & information

Live media, including but not limited to video, audio, and text media that is broadcast and/or shared in real-time, is an emerging form of social media. Live media platforms such as Twitch, Periscope, Meerkat, and Facebook Live are serving as new media places for people to come together, participate in shared experiences, and form communities. In this course, we will explore how live media helps people communicate, work, play, and share.

This is a research-oriented course for advanced students. We will start with foundational computer supported collaborative work (CSCW) literature and move on to explore current literature describing the state-of-the-art. We will work to understand existing live media practice, explore emerging live media contexts such as online education, and investigate new directions for designing collaborative live experiences.

We will build innovative projects intended to develop new research contributions. The hallmark of the course is an extended final project sequence performed by teams or individuals. Bring your passionate interests!

This course is suitable for students with skills in human-computer interaction, cognitive psychology, visualization, digital humanities, communication, engineering, and other relevant disciplines are welcome.

T, Th 12:45 - 2:00, Teague 326

Note: this course is reading and writing intensive
