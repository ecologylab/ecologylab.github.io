user study 1

qualitative experience data gathering

Using the lo-fidelity prototype you constructed in the last deliverable (or a revised version, depending on the feedback you got from me), gather data about how users experience your concept and initial design. Let the scenarios key your inquiry.
Focus on the important details, such as:
  • Introduction -- what the system does, and what the goals are for this study.
  • The tasks that participants are asked to perform / activities they engage in;
  • Significant data that was collected. This is not all the data, necessarily, in raw form, but some digested form that presents everything relevant that happened during the study.
  • Analysis. What was learned? How will this impact the design?
  • The affordances for notification of events in context, and for users' responses;
  • How state is made visible (or audible, or tactile), or not;
  • Flows of essential information, expression, and control;
  • How users transition between real life and the mediated reality (e.g., engagement in interactive i/o while walking down hallways or crossing streets);
  • Social aspects of experience, and how your system affects them.
A suggested approach is to carry out the process described in [2]. Rehearse on each other before you bring in your subject/friends. Realize that lo-fi prototypes can be buggy! Work to identify and fix bugs in advance. Assign the suggested roles -- such as greeter, computer, observer -- to members in your group. Perform a debriefing at the end [2].
Before you conduct your study, you must write up an Application for the Use of Human Subjects in Research form, which you will use to describe your study plan. Along with this, you will turn in an appropriate consent form. Here is a sample . Turn in application and consent form, and get them approved by the class's special IRB (your TA and professor).
