CPSC 610 Hypertext

Professor Andruid Kerne info

T.A. Vikram Sundaram - vikrams@tamu.edu
A survey of research and practice in hypertext systems, hypertext design, and hypertext theory. Approaches to structuring knowledge and information, with links as a key. The origins of the concept of hypertext, and the development of the first hypertext systems in the 1960s. The explosion of hypertext systems in the 1980s. The move to the World Wide Web, and alternative models of hypertext (e.g. spatial hypertext) in the 1990s. Topics include open-hypermedia systems, notions of time and synchronization in hypertext, adaptive hypertext, design methods for hypertexts, and the theoretical implications of hypertext on human communication.

The course will be conducted as a model of research practice.
