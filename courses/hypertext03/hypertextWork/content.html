Professor Andruid Kerne info
Evaluation
15% research analysis + synthesis presentation: oral
15% research analysis + synthesis presentation: written
20% class participation
50% research project(s)
Class Participation: You need to come to class to have a chance to participate. Yet, participation is much more than simply attending class. Participation means being prepared by having done the readings. Participation means taking an active role in discussions. It means thinking critically, yet supportively. Participation is articulating your ideas in the context of a process where we are building ideas together.
Research Project(s): Research projects can be developed by an individual or by a group (3-5 members, with more members indicating a larger project.) Individual student's grades for projects will be influenced by their teamwork as evaluated by the other project group members. Projects can consist of almost anything having to do with hypertext, such as building a new system, adding more advanced hypertext functionality to the Web, evaluating the use/impact of a hypertext, or creating a hypertext for a particular application. Projects must be approved by the instructor. Those students already engaged in hypermedia research are free to use the course as a forum for continuing that research and getting feedback. Be in touch with the instructor as soon as possible with your ideas about the projects you will develop.
