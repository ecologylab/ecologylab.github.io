welcome
This course develops an ecosystems approach to structures of interactive information. These structures are semantic and technical. They are cultural and creative. We will explore programming, design, authoring, and theory. We will work conceptually, visually, and algorithmically.
  • Interactive information as a form of communication.
  • Object-oriented JavaScript + Dynamic HTML.
  • Visual design, including color theory, space, and foreground/background relationships.
  • The design of text and layout, with programming in CSS.
  • Visual and conceptual layering.
  • The reference as a metadisciplinary structure.
  • Concepts of collecting from Duchamp to Berners-Lee to Public Enemy and Moby,
  • Ontologies, maps, and navigation as functional semiotic means for structuring interactive information.
  • Dynamic navigation as a holy grail for addressing the collections problem.
I ask you to create hypermedia projects that are expressive and interpretive.
You will learn to specify problems, as well as to solve them.

This class is listed as CPSC 489 Information Media for Fall 2004.

Professor Andruid Kerne
