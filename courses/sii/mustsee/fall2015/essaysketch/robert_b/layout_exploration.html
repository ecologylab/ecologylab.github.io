Layout Exploration


Welcome to the Layout Exploration page! There exist many proponents of grid-based design and its basic ideas go back thousands of years. Among its advocates are French designer and architect Le Corbusier and American designer Paul Rand.


Paul Rand, a renowned American art director and graphic designer, was a large proponent of grid-based design. His style emphasized cleanliness and readability. The layout exploration page, in one of its variations, presents sentences in a Rand-esque, modular grid. This same basic grid was utilized in his designs for an IBM manual. The design lends itself well to the Exploration Engine.


Le Corbusier, another proponent of grid-based design, emphasized proportions in his grids. His work often utilized the so-called golden ratio, which is found in the Fibonacci sequence. The Layout Exploration, too, makes use of this concept, presenting information in a Le Corbusier-inspired grid.
