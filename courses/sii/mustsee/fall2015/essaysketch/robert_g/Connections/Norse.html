Norse Attack Map

The Norse Attack map is a visual aid for users to see, in real time, the amount of internet attacks that occur. While it is obvious that the site is not able to collect all attacks that ever happened, it is a great representation on the reality of the internet. So much occurs behind the scenes of what most common users do not have the ability to comprehend. Nor do they have any desire to understand. The website is able to track what IP Addresses, the countries, and what type of attacks are being issued.

The map uses color to provide basis of large amounts of information. Tufte talks about strategizing visual cues to reflect large amounts of information, and let the user infer objects by providing a logical guide for them to follow. The attack map uses a black background and a bold teal border for countries. This accentuates the size of the globe, and providing a basis for how insignificant the individual's residence on the planet is. With bold dots constantly flashing resembling attacks, we are reminded that there is much more to our lives than the environments we live in, giving a sense for how small the individual is.

Color the first thing we notice of a new object. It is the driving power of first impressions and immediately creates an attitude for your following perception. Look how color can be used to completely change the view of a web page, and to convey large amounts of information
