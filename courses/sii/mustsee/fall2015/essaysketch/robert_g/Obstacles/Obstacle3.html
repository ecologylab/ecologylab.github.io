It Never Ends

A single red rose in the middle. It’s a nice enough grave. Well, if you like this mixture of dentimeter and cyclamen. But her flowers had already withered. And I planted my red, red rose smack in the middle.



Running away from the old people’s home, paying for the taxi, stumbling to my Joe’s grave in pain. Yes, my Joe. He’d been married to her for nearly fifty years and all. Never had I made contact during that time. Seen him once or twice in her company, but what good did that do? Never had I forgotten him. He was her husband,

Spending my final days in the old people’s home, dwelling on missed opportunities. Now here I am after all this time, at Joe’s grave sulking in pain, an emotional mess. He’d been married to her for nearly fifty years and all. Holding on to love, what good did that do? Never had I forgotten him, but could Joe say the same? He was her husband,



I planted my red, red rose and left. She’ll never know how lucky she truly was. She never knew the true pain of love.

if you know what I mean. And I don’t want to shock her, but she’d better believe it now.
