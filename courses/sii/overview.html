T.A. Madhur Khandelwal - madhur@tamu.edu

The design of information artifacts. Tools, techniques, semiotics, history, and aesthetics of media development. The context of the information age. Media assemblages as a means of expression. Interactivity. Navigation. Text, images, an sound. The link as the form of hypermedia integration. Programming in DHTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Java.

The information age is the period in which media artifacts take center stage. Media are the essential means of communciation. Systems of representation collide, vying for power.

In this class, we're as interested in why?, as in how?. This class is about forming your own position. It's about expressing yourself. What do you stand for? What is important to you? You're learning powerful techniques in many of your classes. What effects can they have? How do you want them to be effective? What do you want to do with them?
