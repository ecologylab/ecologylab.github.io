My goal is to create tools and experiences which empower and inspire.

I am currently a master's student at Texas A&M and member of the Interface Ecology Lab. I am a strong supporter of user-center design. I believe that thoughtfully designed software has incredible potential to change the human experience.

My philosophies and goals have me entangled in many aspects human-computer interaction: information visualization, creativity support, multi-touch interaction, interactive installations, and fashionable interfaces. Specifically my current interests include: semantic zooming, piles, visual snippets, and search and comparison interfaces.

My current goal is to create a new web-browsing experience, by combining traditional browsing and information composition.

In the EcologyLab I am involved in the ongoing development of InfoComposer. My contributions to this project include the design and implementation of the InfoComposer pie menu. I have also worked on the meta-metadata language.
