Ajit Jain

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at TAMU, working on developing new means of assessing multi-scale creative web design. I am co-advised by Dr. Andruid Kerne and Dr. Ruihong Huang. My research interests include information-based ideation, visual design, interaction design, web semantics, sensemaking, and context-aware computing.
My research work involves studying, understanding, and advancing creativity support tools, i.e., innovation for innovation. We strive to make people’s engagement with information more interactive, efficient, and meaningful.

As a part of my Masters thesis, I developed TweetBubble, a free and open source Chrome extension that enables Twitter users to expand @usernames, #hashtags without tabs or windows. Previously, I worked on BigSemanticsService, which is a RESTful interface to the Meta-Metadata structured semantics. I also participated in the development of PhotoNav, a wearable computing investigation for assisting pedestrian navigation.

Prior to joining Interface Ecology lab, I was working with Samsung Electronics, for a period of 4 years, at its India and S. Korea centers. My work there primarily was on Java Virtual Machine graphics and notebook apps. I completed my undergraduate degree in Information Technology, from Delhi College of Engineering, in 2007.
