Oliver Garretson, B.A., Student, Sociology

Oliver Garretson is currently an undergraduate Sociology student at Texas A&M University as well as a student researcher for the Interface Ecology Lab. His research and volunteer work in post-wildfire Bastrop, TX introduced him to the methods of ethnography as well as grounded theory. Interviewing fire survivors and other fieldwork in Bastrop seeded a passion for qualitative methodology.

Oliver worked on an ethnography of Twitch (a popular video game live streaming website). This Twitch ethnography (see publication below), as his introduction into HCI, has widened his sociological experience. Oliver's research interest include exploring computing supported creativity and social interaction across media and cultures.

Currently, Oliver is researching the way people interact with trans-surface technology. Continuing his qualitative work, this research invites couples to redesign a room they live in with mobile devices and a large multi-touch collaborative surface.

publications and presentations

Hamilton, W., Garretson, O., and Kerne, A. Streaming on Twitch: Fostering Participatory Communities of Play within Live Mixed Media, Proc. CHI 2014, Toronto, Canada, April 2014. http://dx.doi.org/10.1145/2556288.2557048
